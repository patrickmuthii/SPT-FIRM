Scalping Strategies at SPT Capital

At SPT Capital, scalping is not guesswork — it is a highly disciplined strategy designed to capture short-term inefficiencies in the market while managing risk with precision. Unlike long-term investing, scalping requires speed, structure, and robust risk management to generate consistent returns without exposing capital recklessly.

1. Market Structure & Liquidity

  • We identify micro-trends and liquidity zones on 1–5 minute charts.
  • Focus on order flow, support/resistance, and liquidity sweeps where price often reacts.
  • Align micro-moves with higher time frame direction to increase probability while limiting exposure.

2. Technical Triggers

  • Use 21 EMA & 9 EMA to track short-term momentum.
  • Stochastic oscillators confirm overbought/oversold conditions for precise entries.
  • Indicators form part of a multi-confirmation system to avoid false signals.

3. Risk Management Discipline

  • Each scalp has a defined stop-loss, typically 3–5 pips (or equivalent in XAU/USD ticks).
  • Risk per trade is capped at a strict percentage of capital to protect portfolio stability.
  • Profit targets are pre-defined, often seeking 2x or greater reward-to-risk ratios.

4. Execution & Psychology

  • Scalping requires fast decision-making, but always follows structured rules.
  • We limit trades per session to maintain quality over quantity.
  • Traders follow strict checklists, minimizing errors and promoting consistency.

Why Investors Benefit from Our Scalping Approach

  • High frequency of opportunities, allowing steady capital compounding.
  • Low overnight risk, since positions are intraday and rarely held across sessions.
  • Diversification, balancing longer-term strategies with a steady tactical edge.

At SPT Capital, scalping is not a gamble — it is a measured, rules-based method that transforms short-term volatility into sustainable growth opportunities for investors.
